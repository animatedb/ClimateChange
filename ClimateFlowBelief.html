How much will temperature rise

The temperature rise is impossible to exactly predict because it depends on so many factors such as the falling costs of solar panels and batteries, possible carbon capture technologies, political changes, etc.

One way to understand it is to try the MIT EN-ROADS simulator.

The best chart I have seen to understand temperature rise is the probability density curve by Wagner&Weitzman that can be found here. 80000hours.org/problem-profiles/climate-change/.


This graph shows that if humans do not mitigate climate change, there is about a 10% chance that the temperature could be above 5 degrees C. This graph shows that lower than .8 degrees rise has zero probability because the rise has already been above .8 degrees. This shows that the most likely rise is about 3 degrees C (5.4 degrees Fahrenheit), but this is very difficult to predict accurately.

What are the predicted effects of climate change

It is impossible to exactly predict all effects of climate change because it is dependent on How much will temperature rise. The effects do not increase linearly with temperature rise and are actually much worse as the temperatures get higher.

Commonly listed fairly direct effects are:

  • Changing temperatures
  • Ocean acidification
Some of the derived effects are:
  • Rising sea level
  • Extreme weather
  • Vector-borne illnesses
  • Food supply
  • Water
  • Human Migration
  • Species loss
